Welcome to NewsMuse! NewsMuse is a prototype of a newspaper design database for newspaper page designers seeking inspiration for their next layout.

I'm Julianne, and I created this site using designs from my own portfolio as a starting point. From 2013 to 2014, I worked as a copy editor and page designer at the Carroll County Times in Westminster, Maryland. In 2015, I joined the copy desk of The Frederick News-Post in Frederick, Maryland, where I worked until 2018. Above is a photo of the FNP copy desk crew in summer 2018. From left are Terence, slayer of poor syntax (slot editor); Ben, trivia whiz (copy editor and page designer); Justin, newsroom clown and world's best manager (copy chief); Joseph, fearless leader and patient teacher (former copy chief); Katlynn, illustrator extraordinaire (copy editor and page designer); and me (copy editor and page designer).

While there are several websites that highlight newspaper design, such as the Society for News Design, News Page Designer, and the Newseum, none of these sites attempts to categorize page designs by their shared visual elements. News Page Designer, for instance, groups designs together by section, such as "online," "news," "sports," "features," "infographics," "illustration," and "magazine." But what if a page designer is looking for examples of creative display headlines or photo illustrations but does not care about the section of the paper in which they appear? NewsMuse aims to provide more granular metadata about the formal elements of page design so that editors working in different departments can learn from one another.
